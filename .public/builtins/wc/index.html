wc

Fri, Aug 20, 2021

wc is a tool for getting the word count from a file or files. It also capable of getting newline, character, and byte counts. wc’s definition of a word is

a non-zero-length sequence of characters delimited by white space.

– Manual page wc(1)

Usage Examples

Print the newline, word, and byte counts of a file (no arguments default).

$ wc file.txt

# Alternatively, cat file.txt | wc

Print only the word count of a text stream.

$ echo one two three | wc -w

Notes

  • Other than words, you can also specify -c, -m, and -l for bytes, chars, and lines respectively.
